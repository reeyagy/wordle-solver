How the solver thinks

01

Every guess yields a pattern

Comparing a guess to any possible answer produces one of 3⁵ = 243 green/yellow/gray patterns. Nothing is graded on being "right" yet — just on what shape of feedback it produces.

02

Score by expected information

For each candidate guess, group the remaining possible answers by the pattern they'd produce, then compute the Shannon entropy of that split: H = −Σ p·log₂(p). Higher entropy means a more even split.

03

Play the highest-entropy guess

The guess that splits the field most evenly is expected to shrink it the most, regardless of the real answer. Filter candidates by the real feedback, and repeat.
